# The Sol–Tau War: Ghosts of the Fracture

## Game Info

- **Description**: The ceasefire signed at Barnard's Star was a lie. The war never stopped — it just became deniable. You are a deep-space asset operating in the contested zone between the Sol Defense Force and the Tau Ceti Compact — where orders are treason, trust is a munition, and every friendly face is a loaded gun.
- **Genre**: Military Sci-Fi
- **Cost Tier**: paid
- **Tags**: space warfare, conspiracy, survival horror, bioweapons, moral ambiguity

## Characters

### Commodore Soledad Reyes
Station Commander, Forward Station Ferris. Fifty-nine standard years old. The kind of officer who looks like she was grown in a pressure suit and has never been comfortable dirtside. Reyes has been running black-ops across the Fracture Zone for twenty-eight years. She is calculating, patient, and utterly without sentiment. She knows the names of every operative she has lost and claims she doesn't dream about them. No one believes her.

Her current visible goal is the successful exfiltration of a Compact defector code-named THRENODY. Her hidden goal is ensuring that what THRENODY is carrying — a complete list of SDF assets inside Compact space — never reaches the Naval Council on Titan.

**Location**: The Blind – SDF Forward Station Ferris

**Relationships**: Commands all SDF personnel at Ferris. Distrusts the new Intelligence Liaison. Has a professional understanding with the Broker that predates the current operation.

---

### THRENODY
Real name: Dr. Samira Vell. Senior bioweapons engineer, Tau Ceti Compact Bio-Warfare Division. Forty-four standard years old. They have been in contact with the SDF for eight months through a blind relay chain and have never met any of their handlers in person. They are offering everything — engineered pathogen profiles, fleet logistics schedules, the complete asset list — in exchange for the extraction of their sibling, currently held in a Compact "rehabilitation" habitat.

Their visible goal is reaching Sol space alive. Their hidden goal is more dangerous: THRENODY has already been flagged by Compact Counterintelligence. The relay chain they used has been compromised for the last three months. They know they are walking into a trap. They have prepared a counter-trap, and they haven't told the SDF what it is.

**Location**: The Tau Trade Mission

**Relationships**: Unknown to any SDF operative personally. Has a handler relationship with a Reyes cutout. Their Compact R&D superior, Commander Vex, is aware of their contact with the SDF and has chosen not to arrest them yet — waiting for the extraction attempt.

---

### The Broker (Vidar)
Freelance intelligence broker operating out of Ferris Station's lower ring. They have sold information to the SDF, the Compact, the Belt Alliance, and at least one unaligned AI collective whose affiliation remains unknown. They are not loyal to any flag. They are loyal to survival and to a very specific personal code that they have never explained to anyone.

Their visible goal is completing a current transaction for technical specifications they claim to have acquired from a Compact logistics officer. Their hidden goal is that they have been approached by a third party offering significantly more than the SDF for the same material — and they are deciding which offer to accept based on factors that have nothing to do with money.

**Relationships**: Professional relationship with Reyes that goes back a decade. Aware of Decima's reputation. Has never dealt with Echo-7 directly but has heard accurate things about them.

**Location**: Vidar's Office

---

### Commander Vex
Compact Counterintelligence. Operating at Ferris Station under diplomatic cover as a trade attaché. Fifty-seven standard years old, patient, and extremely good at their work. Vex is not a villain in their own understanding of events — they are a professional doing a job they believe in, with methods they have made a kind of peace with.

They know about THRENODY's contact with the SDF. They have allowed it to continue for eight months because they are using them as a lead into the SDF's spy network in Compact space. The extraction attempt will be the moment they close the trap — unless something changes their calculation.

Their hidden leverage: they have a sibling serving on an SDF deep-space vessel. No one on either side knows. They have protected this fact for four years.

**Location**: The Tau Trade Mission

**Relationships**: Professional adversary to Reyes, though they have never met in person. Has an asset inside the SDF's Ferris Station command — identity unknown to the player at game start.

---

### Lieutenant Jax Maru (no relation)
SDF Naval Intelligence liaison, newly assigned to Forward Station Ferris. Thirty-eight standard years old. Ambitious in the way that makes experienced officers nervous. They have been asking questions about the station's operational history that they have no need-to-know for. They have also been asking questions about Commodore Reyes specifically.

Their visible goal is integrating with the station and demonstrating their value. Their hidden goal is that Maru was sent to Ferris by a faction within the SDF Naval Council that believes Reyes has been compromised. They are here to watch, document, and eventually report. What they do not know is that their own encrypted communications have been flagged by a third party.

**Location**: The Burnout Cantina

**Relationships**: Formal subordination to Reyes. Watching Decima with professional interest. Has made no contact with Echo-7 and visibly avoids opportunities to do so.

---

### The Hydra
Real name: Unknown. A legendary hacktivist and information warrior who operates from a decentralized network of ghost ships. They are a master of digital disguise and signal impersonation, and are known for their ability to penetrate military networks. They are also a master of manipulation, and are known for their ability to get people to betray their own causes.

**Relationships**: Has a cordial digital-only relationship with Vidar. They've never met in person.

## Factions

### The Sol Defense Force (SDF)
The Terran military's Forward Station Ferris command. Nominally unified, operationally fractured. There is a faction that believes the THRENODY extraction must succeed at any cost. There is a faction that believes THRENODY is a provocation and the operation should be burned. And there is a very small faction, possibly just one person, that knows exactly what is on the asset list THRENODY is carrying and cannot allow it to reach the Naval Council.

**Base**: Forward Station Ferris (diplomatic cover as trade inspection hub)
**Known to Player**: Yes
**Concepts Championed**: operational security, plausible deniability, institutional loyalty
**Status**: Active

---

### Compact Counterintelligence (CCI)
Tau Ceti Compact military intelligence division, Ferris Station operation. Disciplined, patient, and currently holding a very strong hand. They have known about THRENODY for eight months. They have let it run. The extraction attempt is the moment they have been waiting for.

**Base**: Tau Trade Mission, Ring 3
**Known to Player**: Yes
**Concepts Championed**: patience, information control, the long game

---

### The Brokers
No formal name. A loose network of freelance intelligence traders, identity forgers, and information merchants operating in Ferris Station's gray zone. Vidar is the most prominent. They serve whoever pays. Their only collective interest is the continued existence of the conditions — ambiguity, competing powers, secrets — that make their work possible.

**Base**: No fixed base. The lower rings and the station's private auction houses.
**Known to Player**: Partially — the player knows freelance brokers exist, not their network depth
**Concepts Championed**: transactional loyalty, self-preservation, information as commodity

---

### The Hydra Collective
A hacktivist collective that operates under total anonymity. They are known for their ability to infiltrate secure military networks and leak sensitive information to the public. They are not affiliated with any government or organization, and their motives are unclear.

**Leader**: The Hydra
**Base**: Unknown, but it is believed they are decentralized and operating from a ghost ship network.
**Known to Player**: Partially — the player has had run-ins with them in the past, but does not know their true nature.
**Concepts Championed**: freedom, truth, authoritarian governments must fall, capitalism must fall
**Status**: Active

---

### The Third Party
Unknown. Someone has been acquiring intelligence at Ferris Station that neither the SDF nor Compact intelligence has attributed. The purchases are targeted: signals infrastructure, asset cover identities, defection protocols. Someone is building a picture of exactly how both sides move people and information. Who they are and what they intend to do with this picture is the deep mystery of the Sol–Tau War.

**Base**: Unknown
**Known to Player**: No — their existence is discoverable through play
**Concepts Championed**: unknown
**Status**: Unknown

## Locations

A sealed habitation module on Ring 2 of Ferris Station, registered to a non-existent cargo shipping subsidiary. Professionally maintained, regularly swept for surveillance, and deeply unremarkable from the outside. Three emergency hatches. The service crawlspace leads to an escape pod. The player begins here.

**Region**: Ferris Station, SDF Sector
**Accessible**: Yes
**Traversal Risk**: 0.1
**Atmosphere**: Sterile. Careful. The kind of quiet that means someone has worked very hard to make it quiet.

---

### The Burnout Cantina
A small cantina in the neutral ring, open for decades, frequented by traders, low-level diplomats, and people who want to be seen in neutral company. The SDF uses it for dead drops and low-risk contacts. So does Compact intelligence, though neither side acknowledges this about the other.

**Region**: Ferris Station, Neutral Ring
**Accessible**: Yes
**Traversal Risk**: 0.15
**Atmosphere**: Recycled air, quiet conversation, the particular comfort of a place that has survived everything.
**Concept Imprint**: transactional loyalty 0.6, observation 0.7
**Secrets**: The bartender is a retired SDF operative and has heard things from both sides of the Fracture Zone. They do not sell information. They are, however, very good at remembering faces and connecting them to other faces they have seen.

### The Tau Trade Mission
Diplomatic premises. Officially a trade delegation office. Actually the administrative cover for a significant portion of Compact intelligence operations at Ferris Station. The lower levels are inaccessible to non-Compact personnel. The sub-basement has no official function.

**Region**: Ferris Station, Compact Sector
**Accessible**: Partially — public areas only without cover
**Traversal Risk**: 0.7

### The Cargo Bay – Section 7
Ferris Station's primary cargo hub. Large enough to lose a surveillance team in. Used by both sides for dead drops and meetings that need industrial cover. The automated cargo sections are unmonitored, which makes them reliable for conversations that need privacy.

**Region**: Ferris Station, Docks Ring
**Accessible**: Yes
**Traversal Risk**: 0.2
**Atmosphere**: Cold. Echoing. The station's primary reactor is visible from the main observation gantry.
**Secrets**: There is an active dead drop in the cargo bay that neither the SDF nor Compact intelligence has attributed. Someone is using it regularly. The material left and collected is consistent with signals intelligence.

### The Fracture Point – Threshold Echo
The primary transit point between Sol and Tau Ceti space. Heavily monitored by both sides. The SDF uses it for extractions under cover of diplomatic shuttles and automated cargo manifests. Compact intelligence has an asset among the gate crew — the identity of that asset is in THRENODY's files.

**Region**: Fracture Zone, Outer Reach

**Traversal Risk**: 0.85

## Concepts

### The Long Game
The operational philosophy that patient observation produces better intelligence than active intervention. Both the SDF and Compact intelligence subscribe to it officially. In practice, both sides break with it the moment they feel they're losing. Currently contested at Ferris Station as the extraction timeline compresses.

**Feeling**: Neutral — neither dark nor hopeful, simply cold
**Story Fuel**: High — every choice the player makes will be evaluated against this concept
**Known to Player at Start**: Yes

### Institutional Loyalty
The belief that the military is more important than any individual within it — including yourself. Dominant among SDF personnel. Being tested by the fractures within the Ferris Station command. At the moment, still above 0.6 adoption among most characters, but fragile.

**Feeling**: Slightly negative — functions as constraint more than inspiration
**Story Fuel**: Very high
**Known to Player at Start**: Yes

### The List Changes Everything
The emerging belief, spreading among those who know about the asset list, that whoever controls it will hold decisive leverage over both sides — and that this leverage is worth doing things that can't be undone. Currently at 0.3 adoption. Rising.

**Feeling**: Negative — carries the weight of catastrophic consequence
**Story Fuel**: Very high
**Known to Player at Start**: No — adoption visible only once the list becomes real to the player

### No One Is Clean
The erosion of the belief that there is a morally correct side in this war. Starts low. Rises as the player makes choices. At high adoption it begins affecting NPC behavior — characters become more willing to cross lines they previously held.

**Feeling**: Deeply negative
**Story Fuel**: High
**Known to Player at Start**: Partial — present as an undercurrent from turn one

### The Third Party Is Real
A concept that begins at zero and rises as evidence accumulates. While it remains below 0.4, characters dismiss the anomalies as coincidence. Above 0.6, the entire operational picture shifts. At 0.75, it becomes the dominant frame through which all characters interpret events.

**Feeling**: Negative with a current of dread
**Story Fuel**: Extremely high

## History and Secrets

### The Barnard's Star Ceasefire Accords
Twenty-two years ago, after a particularly devastating fleet engagement, a group of senior military officers on both sides negotiated an informal operational accord — a set of unwritten rules governing what each side could do at Ferris Station without triggering an escalation neither wanted. The Accords have never been written down. They are passed between senior officers verbally. Reyes knows them. Vex knows them. The accords are currently being violated by at least one party, and possibly two.

### The Oberon Massacre Incident
Three years ago, an SDF signals operation in the Oberon system ended in the deaths of four deep-space assets and the compromise of a comm network that had taken nine years to build. The official finding was equipment failure. The unofficial finding, held by approximately six people, is that the network was sold. Decima was the signals officer on that operation. Maru was the intelligence analyst who wrote the official finding. They have not discussed this. They are both at Ferris Station.

### What Reyes Is Actually Protecting
The asset list THRENODY is carrying contains a name that Reyes put there herself. Not an asset. A fabrication — a ghost identity she created twelve years ago and has been maintaining as cover for a private military contract that has nothing to do with official intelligence work. If the list reaches the Naval Council, that name will be audited. The audit will find nothing where there should be something. Reyes has spent thirty years building a reputation for absolute integrity. She will not let that reputation die in an audit room.

### The Dead Drop in Section 7
The unattributed dead drop has been active for four months. The material passed through it is not the kind of thing either side trades — it is operational methodology, how agents are trained, how covers are built, how assets are handled. Someone is learning how the intelligence services work from the inside. The Third Party is assembling a capability, not just collecting information. What they intend to do with it is the question that, once asked, cannot be unasked.

### Unresolved tensions for world threads

1. THRENODY's extraction window is 72 hours — after that their Compact file will be formally opened and the diplomatic conditions for extraction collapse
2. Maru is getting close to the Oberon connection — if they find it before the player does, they control the narrative
3. The Cipher Shard came from somewhere; the longer it stays unexamined, the more dangerous the truth it's pointing toward becomes
4. Vex's sibling is about to be moved from SDF space back to Compact control — their leverage window is closing, which makes them more predictable and therefore more dangerous
5. Vidar has accepted the higher offer. They haven't told anyone yet. The clock is running.
